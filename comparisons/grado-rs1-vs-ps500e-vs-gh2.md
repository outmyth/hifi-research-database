# Grado RS1 Vintage vs PS500e vs GH2

## Short Verdict

These three Grados should be kept as different flavors rather than strict upgrades over one another. RS1 Vintage is the classic wooden Grado reference, PS500e is the practical vocal/rock daily Grado, and GH2 is the smoother limited wooden Grado flavor.

## Positioning

| Model | Role | Best Reason to Keep |
|---|---|---|
| RS1 Vintage | Classic wooden Grado | Vintage tone, collector feel, rock/vocal intimacy |
| PS500e | Practical metal-cup Grado | Easy vocal/rock use, fuller body, good local value |
| GH2 | Limited wooden Grado | Smoother wood tone, Heritage-series interest |

## Sound Comparison

| Area | RS1 Vintage | PS500e | GH2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bass | Punchy, not deep | Fuller and grounded | Natural and moderate |
| Midrange | Forward, classic, vivid | Forward and practical | Smooth woody intimacy |
| Treble | Lively | Energetic but often more grounded | Lively but smoother |
| Stage | Intimate | Intimate | Intimate |
| Character | Vintage excitement | Practical body | Limited smooth wood flavor |

## Ownership Logic

### Keep RS1 Vintage if:

- You want the most classic wooden Grado identity.
- You value vintage/collector tone.
- You want the strongest emotional Grado reference.

### Keep PS500e if:

- You want a practical easy-drive Grado for vocals and rock.
- You value its fuller metal-cup body.
- You want a less precious daily Grado.

### Keep GH2 if:

- You want a smoother limited wooden Grado.
- You value Heritage-series scarcity.
- It gives a different tone from RS1 Vintage in direct listening.

## Vincent Decision Rule

- Do not sell a Grado until it has been directly compared against the other two with the same pads/source.
- If simplifying later: keep RS1 Vintage for collector value, then choose between PS500e practicality and GH2 limited wood flavor.
- None of these replace HD800, LCD-3, or LCD-X roles; they are intimate rock/vocal tools.
