# Koss ESP/950 vs Koss ESP/95X

## Short Verdict

ESP/950 and ESP/95X are best treated as very close siblings. For pure sound-per-dollar, ESP/95X can be excellent; for Vincent's collector logic, original ESP/950 is more desirable if a complete system appears around $300-$350.

## Positioning

| Model | Position | Best Reason to Buy |
|---|---|---|
| ESP/950 | Original classic Koss electrostatic system | Collector value, old-school appeal, completeness |
| ESP/95X | Drop/Massdrop version | Value, accessibility, similar electrostatic flavor |

## Sound Difference Summary

Reported differences are usually subtle and may be caused by:

- Pad condition
- Production variation
- Age and driver tension differences
- Energizer condition
- Expectation bias
- Accessory/package differences

Some older enthusiasts report ESP/950 as warmer, thicker, or more organic. Many technical listeners consider the two close enough that pad and condition matter more than model name.

## Why Some Old-School Listeners Prefer ESP/950

- Original model has stronger historical identity.
- Older production may sound slightly warmer or softer.
- More complete original package can feel more collectible.
- Long-term Head-Fi nostalgia favors the original.

## Why ESP/95X Still Makes Sense

- Often easier to find.
- Usually cheaper.
- Provides most of the Koss electrostatic experience.
- Good enough as a reference point before buying ESP/950.

## Buying Recommendation for Vincent

### Add ESP/950 if:

- Complete system is <= $350.
- Around $300 and fully working.
- Seller confirms both channels work.
- E/90 energizer is included and healthy.
- It is not a for-parts or untested listing.

### Do not add ESP/950 if:

- Price is $500+ and only marginally different from owned ESP/95X.
- Energizer is missing.
- Condition is unclear.
- Listing relies only on nostalgia pricing.

## Database Decision

- ESP/950: higher collector priority.
- ESP/95X: owned/reference electrostatic flavor.
- Buying another Koss electrostatic only makes sense if ESP/950 is clearly priced below normal market.
