# Sennheiser HD800

## Summary

A landmark open-back dynamic headphone with enormous soundstage, high resolution, and strong long-term reference value. For Vincent, HD800 is already an owned system anchor and comparison baseline.

## Status

- Category: Open-back dynamic headphone
- Collector tier: SS
- Current ownership: Owned
- Priority: Keep as reference baseline

## Version / Identification

- Vincent owns an HD800 with serial roughly in the 30k range.
- Stock 6.35 mm cable currently noted.
- Pads/headband wear should be tracked because they materially affect sound and comfort.

## Sound Signature

### Bass

Clean and extended but not physically heavy. Not a slam-first headphone.

### Midrange

Open, clear, and spacious. Can sound lean depending on chain.

### Treble

Highly detailed and sometimes bright. Chain and pad condition matter.

### Soundstage / Imaging

The defining trait: very large, open, and precise.

### Dynamics / Speed

Fast and articulate, but not as weighty as planar headphones like LCD-3/LCD-X.

## Head-Fi Consensus

### Common Praise

- Legendary soundstage
- High resolution
- Excellent imaging
- Long-term reference status

### Common Criticism

- Treble peak can be fatiguing
- Bass impact is limited
- Requires careful amp/source matching
- Pads strongly affect sound

## Reliability / Known Issues

- Pads deteriorate and change tuning.
- Headband pad wear is common.
- Check mesh and driver cleanliness.
- Cable connector handling requires care.

## Buying / Ownership Guide

### Must Check

- Pad condition
- Headband condition
- Driver balance
- Connector condition
- Cable inclusion

### Maintenance Notes

- Replace worn pads with appropriate Sennheiser pads.
- Track pad part numbers and fitment.
- Avoid aggressive cleaning near driver mesh.
