# Sennheiser IE900

## Summary

Vincent's high-end dynamic-driver IEM reference. IE900 is technically strong, spacious, and highly resolving, but source and tip synergy are critical because it can sound thin or metallic from lean pairings.

## Status

- Category: Single dynamic-driver IEM
- Collector tier: S
- Current ownership: Owned
- Priority: Keep as flagship IEM reference

## Ownership Notes

- Purchased from Head-Fi Canada around 2026-05 for approximately $750 shipped.
- Unit was a new replacement with very low hours.
- Seller noted that Hugo 2 / DC Elite could sound thin or metallic, while Mojo 2 and warmer sources were better.
- Vincent first heard it from NiPO A100 as somewhat thin.
- Planned warmer pairings include Sony WM1ZM2 and Mojo/Hugo-family alternatives.

## Sound Signature

### Bass

Deep, clean, and sub-bass capable, but seal and tip choice matter greatly.

### Midrange

Clear and slightly lean depending on source/tips.

### Treble

Highly detailed and energetic. Can become metallic or sharp with lean chains.

### Soundstage / Imaging

Excellent spatial presentation for an IEM.

### Dynamics / Speed

Fast, clean, and technically strong.

## System Synergy

### With Sony WM1ZM2

Primary recommended warm portable pairing. Use this when IE900 sounds thin.

### With Chord Hugo

Technically strong but may risk thin/metallic tone depending on music and tips.

### With Tips

Tip selection is crucial. Track Divinus Velvet, Velvet Wide Bore, AZLA MAX, and other tip notes here.

## Reliability / Known Issues

- MMCX connector care is important.
- Tip fit/seal changes sound dramatically.
- Confirm authenticity and serial/support status for used purchases.
