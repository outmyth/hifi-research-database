# Chord Hugo

## Summary

A portable/transportable Chord DAC/amp reference in Vincent's system. It is important as a technical, resolving Chord source and as a point of comparison against warmer Sony and classic desktop chains.

## Status

- Category: Portable DAC / headphone amplifier
- Collector tier: A
- Current ownership: Owned
- Priority: Keep as Chord reference

## System Role

### General Character

- Chord-style clarity, timing, and resolution.
- More technical/precise than many warm DAPs.
- Useful as a transportable source for headphones and IEMs.

### Strengths

- Resolution and timing
- Compact high-end DAC/amp role
- Useful comparison point against Mojo, Qutest, and Sony WM1ZM2

### Limitations

- Battery/charging behavior should be tracked.
- May sound leaner or more explicit with certain IEMs/headphones.
- Not as warm and forgiving as WM1ZM2.

## System Synergy

### With IE900

Can be technically impressive but may risk sounding thin/metallic depending on pairing and listener preference.

### With HD800

Useful for clarity and stage, but amp pairing and tonal balance matter.

### With Warm Headphones

Likely useful with warmer or denser headphones where Chord precision adds focus.

## Reliability / Ownership Notes

- Track battery and charging LED behavior.
- Monitor charging port and power stability.
- Keep notes on which headphones sound too lean or too sharp from Hugo.
