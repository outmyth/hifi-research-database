# Audeze LCD-2 Classic (LCD-2C)

## Summary

A value-oriented modern Audeze with strong planar body and a darker, relaxed signature. For Vincent, LCD-2C is attractive only when priced very well, because LCD-3 and LCD-X occupy more important long-term roles.

## Status

- Category: Open-back planar magnetic headphone
- Collector tier: A-
- Current ownership: Target only at strong value price
- Priority: Medium

## Position in Audeze Lineup

- More affordable and simplified than LCD-2 wood-cup variants.
- Often valued for classic Audeze warmth and bass weight.
- Less collectible than LCD-2 Rev.2 or LCD-3.

## Sound Signature

### Bass

Full and weighty, with satisfying planar body.

### Midrange

Warm and relaxed, but not as refined or liquid as LCD-3.

### Treble

Dark/smooth. Can be forgiving but may lack excitement for some metal recordings.

### Soundstage / Imaging

Moderate and intimate. Not a stage-focused headphone.

### Dynamics / Speed

Solid, but less precise than LCD-X.

## System Synergy

- Good contrast to HD800's brightness/space.
- May overlap with LCD-3 target but at lower cost.
- Less urgent if LCD-3 is still the main Audeze goal.

## Head-Fi Consensus

### Common Praise

- Strong value
- Classic dark Audeze warmth
- Comfortable enough for long listening if weight is acceptable
- Good entry into Audeze planar sound

### Common Criticism

- Darker tuning
- Less technical than LCD-X
- Less refined than LCD-3
- Lower collector value than wood-cup models

## Reliability / Known Issues

- Check pads and headband.
- Confirm driver balance.
- B-stock can be excellent value but should be checked carefully.
- Case/accessories affect used value.

## Buying Guide

### Must Check

- Driver balance
- Pad condition
- Headband condition
- Case/cable inclusion
- B-stock or standard retail status

### Nice to Have

- Original case
- Warranty or recent purchase proof
- Very low hours

### Avoid

- Pricing close to LCD-X or LCD-3
- Worn pads at high price
- Driver imbalance
