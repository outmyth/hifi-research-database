# Grado GH2

## Summary

A limited wooden Grado in Vincent's collection, useful as a smoother and more relaxed Grado flavor compared with RS1 Vintage and PS500e. It should be kept as part of the Grado tone library unless it becomes redundant after direct comparison.

## Status

- Category: Open-back dynamic headphone
- Collector tier: A
- Current ownership: Owned
- Priority: Keep as limited wooden Grado reference

## Position in Collection

- Represents a limited Heritage-series Grado flavor.
- Sits between vintage RS1-style energy and more practical PS500e use.
- Useful for rock, vocal, and softer sessions where Grado immediacy is desired without maximum aggression.

## Sound Signature

### Bass

Punchy and natural, not sub-bass heavy.

### Midrange

Forward, woody, and intimate, but generally smoother than the most aggressive Grado presentations.

### Treble

Lively but often perceived as less piercing than some classic Grados.

### Soundstage / Imaging

Intimate and direct, with Grado-style immediacy.

### Dynamics / Speed

Fast, engaging, and guitar-friendly.

## System Synergy

- Complements HD800 and Audeze targets by offering intimate wood-tone immediacy.
- Should be directly compared with RS1 Vintage and PS500e for long-term role.
- Works well when a lighter, more immediate headphone is preferred.

## Head-Fi Consensus

### Common Praise

- Smooth wooden Grado tone
- Attractive limited/Heritage identity
- Strong vocals and guitars
- More relaxed than some brighter Grados

### Common Criticism

- Still limited by Grado comfort/stage
- Not as historically important as HP1000 or vintage RS1
- Less technical than larger planar/dynamic references

## Reliability / Known Issues

- Check wooden cups and finish.
- Check cable strain.
- Pads affect sound strongly.
- Headband and rod blocks should be checked.
