# Grado RS1 Vintage

## Summary

A classic wooden Grado and one of Vincent's owned reference headphones for old-school rock, vocal intimacy, and Grado house sound. It is not a technical all-rounder, but it is important as a tone and collector reference.

## Status

- Category: Open-back dynamic headphone
- Collector tier: S
- Current ownership: Owned
- Priority: Keep as vintage Grado reference

## Position in Collection

- Represents vintage wooden Grado character.
- Complements HD800 by offering a much more intimate and forward presentation.
- Important reference for rock, vocal, and guitar-driven music.

## Sound Signature

### Bass

Punchy and quick rather than deep or sub-bass heavy.

### Midrange

Forward, immediate, and emotionally direct. This is the main reason to own vintage RS1.

### Treble

Energetic and lively; can be recording-dependent.

### Soundstage / Imaging

Intimate rather than wide. The presentation is close and engaging.

### Dynamics / Speed

Fast and lively, especially for guitars and drums.

## System Synergy

- Good contrast to HD800 and Audeze targets.
- Should be compared against PS500e and GH2 for Grado roles.
- Works best when a direct, energetic sound is desired.

## Head-Fi Consensus

### Common Praise

- Classic wooden Grado tone
- Engaging mids
- Excellent for rock and vocals
- Strong collector nostalgia

### Common Criticism

- Comfort can be polarizing
- Limited soundstage
- Treble can be lively
- Not a bass-heavy headphone

## Reliability / Known Issues

- Check wood cups for cracks or finish damage.
- Check cable strain.
- Pads strongly affect sound.
- Headband and rod blocks should be inspected.

## Buying / Ownership Guide

### Must Check

- Cup condition
- Cable condition
- Driver balance
- Pad type and condition
- Version/vintage details

### Nice to Have

- Original box
- Good pads
- Clean lettering/buttons depending on version

### Avoid

- Driver imbalance
- Damaged cable
- Cracked cups
- Overpaying purely for vague vintage claims
