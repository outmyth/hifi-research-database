# Ultrasone Pro 900

## Summary

A cult-classic aggressive V-shaped closed-back headphone, historically associated with energetic metal/electronic listening on Head-Fi. For Vincent, it is a specialist flavor rather than a universal reference.

## Status

- Category: Closed-back dynamic headphone
- Collector tier: SS
- Current ownership: Target / watchlist
- Priority: High at <= $180

## Preferred Version

- First-generation Pro 900 is preferred for collector interest and historical Head-Fi reputation.
- Pro 900i may be considered if cheap and clean, but the original version is more interesting for this database.

## Sound Signature

### Bass

Big, punchy, and aggressive. The main reason to own it.

### Midrange

Recessed relative to bass and treble. Not ideal for natural vocals.

### Treble

Bright, energetic, and sometimes sharp. Can help metal excitement but may become fatiguing.

### Soundstage / Imaging

S-Logic presentation can sound spacious for a closed-back headphone, but fit and listener perception vary.

### Dynamics / Speed

High-energy and immediate. Strong fun factor.

## System Synergy

- Complements HD800 and LCD-3 by offering a closed-back, aggressive, fun signature.
- Not a replacement for natural timbre or balanced reference listening.
- Best treated as a metal/energy specialist.

## Head-Fi Consensus

### Common Praise

- Big bass
- Exciting V-shaped tuning
- Fun for metal/electronic music
- Distinctive closed-back presentation

### Common Criticism

- Recessed mids
- Bright/fatiguing treble
- Fit-dependent S-Logic effect
- Not neutral

## Reliability / Known Issues

- Check pads for wear and flattening.
- Check detachable cable and connector.
- Confirm both drivers are balanced.
- Original case and accessories improve value.

## Buying Guide

### Must Check

- Original Pro 900 vs Pro 900i identification
- Driver balance
- Pad condition
- Cable included
- Case included

### Nice to Have

- Original hard case
- Spare pads
- Original cable
- Clean cosmetic condition

### Avoid

- Missing cable at normal price
- Driver rattle
- Excessively worn pads
- Overpriced listings using old MSRP as justification
