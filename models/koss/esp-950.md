# Koss ESP/950

## Summary

A classic American electrostatic headphone system with strong collector appeal. For Vincent, it is a high-priority buy only at the right used-market price, ideally around $300-$350 complete.

## Status

- Category: Electrostatic headphone system
- Collector tier: SSS
- Current ownership: Target / watchlist
- Priority: Very high at target price

## Version / Identification

- Original ESP/950 system typically includes the headphone and E/90 energizer.
- Older ESP/950 units are often preferred by some long-term enthusiasts over the Drop ESP/95X due to perceived tonal richness and classic status.
- Must distinguish complete systems from headphone-only or for-parts listings.

## Sound Signature

### Bass

Fast and clean, but not physically powerful. Not a bass-head or slam-focused headphone.

### Midrange

Smooth, open, and easy to listen to. This is the main appeal.

### Treble

Airy and electrostatic in character, generally less sharp than some Stax setups.

### Soundstage / Imaging

Open and spacious, but not as huge as HD800.

### Dynamics / Speed

Fast transient response, but limited macrodynamic impact compared with stronger planar or dynamic headphones.

## System Synergy

- Complements Vincent's planar and dynamic headphones by adding electrostatic speed and openness.
- Not a replacement for LCD-3, LCD-X, or Pro 900 for metal impact.
- Should be treated as a classic electrostatic flavor and collector piece.

## Head-Fi Consensus

### Common Praise

- Smooth electrostatic presentation
- Excellent midrange clarity
- Long-term comfort in sound
- Great value when bought used

### Common Criticism

- Weak bass impact
- Plastic build
- E/90 energizer is not a high-end electrostatic amp
- Can sound polite for aggressive music

## ESP/950 vs ESP/95X Notes

- Many users treat ESP/950 and ESP/95X as closely related or essentially similar at the driver level.
- Some older enthusiasts still prefer ESP/950 for perceived warmth, thickness, or older-production character.
- Pad condition, age, and system variation can explain much of the reported difference.

## Reliability / Known Issues

- Confirm both channels work equally.
- Check for imbalance, buzzing, or intermittent sound.
- Confirm E/90 energizer powers on and is quiet.
- Avoid untested or for-parts units unless the price is extremely low and risk is accepted.

## Buying Guide

### Must Check

- Complete system: headphone + E/90 energizer
- Channel balance
- No crackle or intermittent connection
- Cable and connector condition
- Seller description clarity

### Nice to Have

- Original case/box
- Manual/accessories
- Clean pads
- Known service history

### Avoid

- For-parts listings
- Untested listings at normal used price
- Missing energizer unless priced as headphone-only
- One-side failure
