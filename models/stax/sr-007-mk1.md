# Stax SR-007 Mk1

## Summary

A major Stax electrostatic classic and one of the most serious long-term electrostatic targets in the database. For Vincent, SR-007 Mk1 is a higher-end electrostatic collector target than Koss ESP/950, but it requires a much more careful amplifier and ownership plan.

## Status

- Category: Open-back electrostatic headphone / earspeaker
- Collector tier: SSS
- Current ownership: Not owned
- Priority: Long-term target if price and system plan are right

## Position in Collection

- Represents the serious Stax flagship electrostatic line.
- More ambitious and system-dependent than Koss ESP/950.
- Not a casual impulse buy; energizer/amp pairing is essential.

## Sound Signature

### Bass

Generally more substantial and serious than lightweight entry electrostatics, but still dependent on proper amplification.

### Midrange

Dense, smooth, and refined when properly driven.

### Treble

Detailed and extended without necessarily being as sharp as some brighter electrostatic models.

### Soundstage / Imaging

More enveloping and layered than simple wide-stage presentation. Not an HD800-style stage; more immersive and controlled.

### Dynamics / Speed

Strong electrostatic speed with more body than many lighter stat designs. Macrodynamic satisfaction depends heavily on amp quality.

## System Synergy

- Requires a compatible Stax Pro-bias electrostatic amplifier/energizer.
- Should not be evaluated from weak or mismatched energizers.
- Would become a new electrostatic reference above ESP/95X and ESP/950.

## Head-Fi Consensus

### Common Praise

- Classic flagship Stax status
- Dense and refined electrostatic presentation
- Excellent long-term ownership appeal
- Scales strongly with amplification

### Common Criticism

- Amp-dependent
- Fit/seal dependent
- Expensive relative to entry electrostatics
- Buying used requires careful channel/cable/driver checks

## Reliability / Known Issues

- Check channel balance.
- Check for imbalance, squeal, buzzing, or intermittent signal.
- Check cable condition and strain relief.
- Confirm bias compatibility.
- Pads/seal matter for bass and tonal balance.

## Buying Guide

### Must Check

- Exact Mk1 identification
- Driver/channel balance
- Cable condition
- Pad condition and seal
- Seller history
- Amp/energizer plan

### Nice to Have

- Original case/box
- Clean pads
- Known ownership history
- Domestic sale to reduce shipping risk

### Avoid

- Unclear Mk1/Mk2 claims
- Channel imbalance
- Hum/buzz/squeal
- Damaged cable
- Buying without an amp plan
